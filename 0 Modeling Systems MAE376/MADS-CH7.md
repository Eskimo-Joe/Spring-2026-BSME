Modeling fluid systems

hydraulic, pneumatic, and thermal systems

key difference, hydraulic is incompressible, pneumatic is compressible

like mechanical systems, fluid systems will be modeled into the electrical equivalent

input of a fluid system should be volumetric flow and output will be a pressure difference

system blocks

hydraulic resistance will be the pressure drop across a valve or constriction
$$P_{1}-P_{2}=Rq$$
hydraulic capacitance will be a fluid reservoir
$$C=\frac{dV}{dP}\to C \frac{dP}{dt}=\frac{dV}{dt}=q_{1}-q_{2}$$
$$C \frac{dP}{dt}=q_{1}-q_{2}$$
$$C=\frac{A}{\rho g}$$

for a tank where the outlet has resistance R
$$q_{out}=\frac{\rho gh}{R}$$
$$q_{in}-\frac{\rho gh}{R}=C \frac{d(\rho gh)}{dt}$$

hydraulic inertance is equivalent to electrical inductance
$$F_{1}-F_{2}=ma$$
$$P_{1}A-P_{2}A=ma$$
$$P_{1}A-P_{2}A=\rho AL \frac{dV}{dt}$$
$$\frac{\rho L}{A} \frac{dq}{dt}=P_{1}-P_{2}$$
inertance (I)
$$I= \frac{\rho L}{A}$$
$$I \frac{dq}{dt}=P_{1}-P_{2}$$
